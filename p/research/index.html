Research

My primary research focus revolves around developing advanced numerical techniques for modeling nematic liquid crystals and other phase field models. Specifically, my work is in constructing robust numerical methods for simulating nematic liquid crystals utilizing a Q-tensor model. Additionally, I've undertaken projects involving numerical methods for the Cahn-Hilliard phase field model.
A notable challenge in this research lies in the balance between the precision and computational efficiency of the numerical schemes, while also preserving the essential physical properties inherent to the model. For example, we may design a scheme that guarantees decreasing energy while also minimizing numerical dissipation to accurately capture the dynamic behavior of nematic liquid crystals and other phase field systems.
