The Ten Year Reading List

In Feb. 2024 I purchased the 54-volume Britanica Great Books set at Recycled Books in Denton, TX. The first volume, The Great Conversation, proposes an interesting take on the western canon that the greatest books of all time present a 2500-year long conversation.

The author of this first volume, Robert M. Hutchins, gives us a ten year reading list to march through the Classics in a way that he has learned works best through his teaching experience. The goal is to provide the reader with the beginnings of a liberal education, which he argues is a lifelong commitment to learning.

Below is this list, originally published in 1952. I plan to update this list with my progress after starting in Feb. 2024.

  • Year One
    • Plato: Apology, Crito
    • Aristophanes: Clouds, Lysistrata
    • Plato: Republic Book I-II
    • Aristotle: Ethics Book I
    • Aristotle: Politics Book I
    • Plutarch: The Lives of the Noble Grecians and Romans
    • New Testament: Matthew, Acts
    • St. Augustine: Confessions Book I-VIII
    • Machiavelli: The Prince
    • Rabelais: Gargantua and Pantagruel Book I-II
    • Montaigne: Essays
      • Of Custom
      • Of Pedantry
      • Of the Education
      • That it is Folly to Measure Truth and Error by Our Own Capacity
      • Of Cannibals
      • That the Relish of Good and Evil Depends ...
      • Upon Some Verses of Virgil
    • Shakespeare: Hamlet
    • Locke: Concerning Civil Government Second Essay
    • Rousseau: The Social Contract Book I-II
    • Gibbon: The Decline and Fall of the Roman Empire Ch. 15-16
    • The Declaration of Independence, The Constitution of the United States, The Federalist Papers Numbers 1-10, 15, 31, 47, 51, 68-71
    • Smith: The Wealth of Nations Intro, Book I Ch. 9
    • Marx-Engels: Manifesto of the Communist Party
